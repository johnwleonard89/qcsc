Mistakes I See Cincinnati Homeowners Make

After 15+ years in the Cincinnati market, I've seen the same mistakes repeated. Here's how to avoid the most common (and costly) errors:

‚ùå

Choosing Based on Price Alone

What Happens:

Homeowner picks $800 DIY kit or lowest contractor bid, coating fails within 2-3 years

Real Cincinnati Example:

"Mason homeowner saved $1,200 on initial install, spent $3,500 to strip and redo properly"

How to Avoid:

  • Get at least 3 quotes and compare systems, not just prices
  • Ask for references from projects 3+ years old
  • Calculate total cost of ownership, not just installation cost
‚ùå

Ignoring Cincinnati's Climate

What Happens:

Beautiful coating cracks during first winter or turns yellow from garage door UV exposure

Real Cincinnati Example:

"West Chester garage with south-facing door - DIY epoxy completely yellowed in 6 months"

How to Avoid:

  • Tell contractor about UV exposure through doors/windows
  • Specify freeze-thaw resistance requirements
  • Consider humidity impact on cure times and adhesion
‚ùå

DIY Without Proper Equipment

What Happens:

Inadequate surface prep leads to coating peeling up in sheets within months

Real Cincinnati Example:

"Fairfield homeowner acid-etched only, coating peeled off when they pulled into garage"

How to Avoid:

  • Surface preparation requires diamond grinding, not just cleaning
  • Professional mixers ensure proper chemical ratios
  • Experience matters for Ohio's challenging conditions
‚ùå

Installing During Wrong Season

What Happens:

Poor cure due to temperature/humidity, adhesion problems, extended downtime

Real Cincinnati Example:

"December installation in unheated garage - coating never fully cured, stayed tacky"

How to Avoid:

  • Best months: March-May, September-November
  • Winter installs need heating and extended cure times
  • Summer humidity requires dehumidification
