Step-by-Step Cleaning Process

1

Remove Loose Debris

Sweep or vacuum the entire floor to remove dirt, dust, and loose debris. Pay special attention to corners and edges where debris accumulates.

Pro Tip: Use a microfiber dust mop for best results on smooth surfaces.
2

Spot Clean Spills

Immediately clean any spills or stains with appropriate cleaner and a microfiber cloth. Blot don't rub to avoid spreading.

Remember: The faster you clean spills, the easier they are to remove.
3

Quick Inspection

Take a moment to visually inspect the floor for any new damage, wear patterns, or areas needing attention.

Look For: Scratches, stains, wear patterns, or coating damage.
1

Prepare the Area

Remove all moveable items and sweep/vacuum thoroughly. Ensure good ventilation if using stronger cleaners.

2

Mix Cleaning Solution

Mix pH-neutral cleaner according to manufacturer directions. Use warm (not hot) water for better cleaning action.

3

Mop Thoroughly

Start from the far end and work toward the exit. Use a clean mop and change water if it becomes too dirty.

4

Address Stains

Use appropriate stain removers for any stubborn spots. Allow dwell time as needed, then clean with fresh water.

5

Final Rinse

Rinse with clean water to remove any cleaner residue. Dry with clean towels or allow to air dry completely.

1

Complete Deep Clean

Follow the weekly cleaning process but with more attention to detail and harder-to-reach areas.

2

Detailed Inspection

Examine the entire floor systematically for wear patterns, damage, or areas needing repair.

3

Document Findings

Take photos of any issues and note their location and severity. This creates a maintenance history.

4

Address Minor Issues

Handle small repairs, touch-ups, or treatments that can be done without professional help.

5

Plan Professional Service

Schedule any professional maintenance or repairs needed based on your inspection findings.

1

Assess the Situation

Determine the type and extent of the spill or damage. Ensure safety first - ventilate area if needed.

2

Contain the Spill

Prevent spreading by containing the spill with absorbent materials or barriers around the affected area.

3

Remove Excess Material

Blot up liquid spills or scrape up solid materials. Don't rub - this can drive contaminants deeper.

4

Clean with Appropriate Solution

Use the right cleaner for the type of spill. Test in an inconspicuous area first if using a new product.

5

Rinse and Assess Damage

Rinse thoroughly and check if the coating has been damaged. Contact professionals if damage is present.
