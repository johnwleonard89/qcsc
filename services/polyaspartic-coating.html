Frequently Asked Questions

Expert answers to common questions about polyaspartic coating technology, installation, and performance.

What is polyaspartic coating and how does it differ from epoxy?

Polyaspartic coating is an advanced aliphatic polyurea technology that cures much faster than epoxy (hours vs. days), offers superior UV resistance, and maintains flexibility at extreme temperatures. Unlike epoxy, polyaspartic doesn't yellow over time and can be installed year-round regardless of temperature. The aliphatic chemistry provides permanent UV stability while the flexible polymer backbone accommodates thermal movement without cracking.

How quickly can I return to service after polyaspartic coating installation?

Polyaspartic coatings typically allow return to service within 2-4 hours for light traffic and 24 hours for heavy traffic and vehicles. This dramatically reduces downtime compared to traditional epoxy systems that require 3-7 days for full cure. The fast cure time is due to moisture-catalyzed chemical reaction that's independent of temperature, allowing same-day completion for most projects.

What are the temperature limits for polyaspartic coating installation?

Polyaspartic coatings can be installed in temperatures from 20¬∞F to 120¬∞F (-7¬∞C to 49¬∞C), making year-round installation possible. The coating also performs in service temperatures from -30¬∞F to 140¬∞F (-34¬∞C to 60¬∞C) without cracking or losing adhesion. This wide temperature range is due to the flexible polymer structure that accommodates thermal expansion and contraction.

How does polyaspartic coating perform outdoors compared to epoxy?

Polyaspartic coatings excel in outdoor applications due to their aliphatic chemistry, providing complete UV resistance without yellowing or degradation. They maintain color stability and physical properties for decades, while epoxy coatings typically fail within 1-2 years when exposed to direct sunlight. The UV stability comes from the aliphatic backbone that doesn't absorb UV radiation, preventing polymer degradation.

What makes polyaspartic coating more flexible than epoxy?

Polyaspartic coatings achieve 45% elongation compared to epoxy's 5%, due to their flexible polymer chains that can stretch without breaking. This flexibility allows the coating to accommodate substrate movement, thermal cycling, and impact without cracking. The flexible structure also provides better impact resistance and prevents the brittleness that can affect rigid epoxy coatings over time.

Are polyaspartic coatings suitable for industrial chemical exposure?

Yes, polyaspartic coatings provide excellent chemical resistance to acids, bases, solvents, and automotive fluids. The cross-linked polymer structure prevents chemical penetration while the aliphatic chemistry resists degradation from harsh chemicals. They're particularly effective against automotive fluids, making them ideal for service centers, manufacturing facilities, and chemical processing areas.

How do polyaspartic coatings compare in cost to epoxy systems?

While polyaspartic coatings have higher initial material costs, they provide superior value through reduced installation time, minimal downtime costs, longer service life (25+ years vs. 15-20 for epoxy), and eliminated UV degradation maintenance. The total cost of ownership is typically lower due to reduced labor costs and extended replacement cycles.

What certifications and testing validate polyaspartic performance?

Our polyaspartic systems meet ASTM standards for tensile strength, adhesion, and abrasion resistance. They're NSF/ANSI 61 certified for drinking water contact, USDA approved for food service areas, and GREENGUARD Gold certified for indoor air quality. The systems also comply with FDA, OSHA, and ACI standards for commercial and industrial applications.
